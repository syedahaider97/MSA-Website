Prayer

The MSA has made arrangements to make prayer on campus easy!


Daily Prayer Room: B90

Throughout the day, B90 - The Reflection Room - has been made available for Muslims on campus to conduct their daily prayers. Jamaats usually take place before or after regular class times. If you find that the room is locked, please ask to have it opened by Campus Center front desk.


Jumma

We have two Jumma congregations every Friday at 12:20 and 1:30. All of our Khateebs are scholars from the local community or trained students from amongst our general body.
If you are interested in being a Khateeb, please email Ali Farhat.

We recommend making Ablution in the basement bathrooms. They are located by the lockers adjacent to B35.

Frequently Asked Questions

FAQ


Is the MSA open to anyone?
Yes! Regardless of your religious background, our events are open for anyone to participate in. We strive to create as welcoming of an atmosphere as possible, and pride ourselves on inclusiveness.
Is there any formal process to join the MSA?
Unlike other clubs, the MSA has elected not to impose a formal process. All of our events and meetings are walk-in, and anyone is free to join!

Is there any commitment to remain a member?
Nope! As college students, we understand that scheduling can be difficult at times. We hope that you join us for as many events as possible, but there is no imposed minimum to remain a member.
Is there any membership fee to join the MSA?
Nope! Unless otherwise stated, all of our events and services are offered to the public free of charge!

How many attendees does the MSA regularly have at events?
While it can be difficult to put an exact number on it, our events usually bring in 70-120 people. However, larger scaled functions have seen numbers from 200-500!
Will the MSA make arrangements for Ramadan?
While Ramadan has not coincided with the semester in the past few years, the MSA always makes arrangements for prominent religious events, and will likely do the same for the upcoming Ramadan, Inshallah.
